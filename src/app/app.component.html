Evaluation Strategies


Lexical + Strict

In lexical (static) scope, each identifier gets its value from the scope of its definition, not its use. In strict (eager) evaluation strategy, an expression is evaluated as soon as it is bound to a variable. Examples: Scheme, OCaml

Dynamic + Strict

Each identifier gets its value from the scope of its use, not its definition. Examples: Bash, Emacs Lisp

Lexical + Lazy

Delays evaluation untill needed (call by name), follows call by value convention. Examples: ALGOL 60, Haskell (call by need)
